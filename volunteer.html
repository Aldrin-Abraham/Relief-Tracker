Volunteer Opportunities

Relief Tracker relies on dedicated volunteers to help us respond to disasters quickly and effectively. Whether you're on the front lines or supporting remotely, your skills can save lives. No prior experience is required for many roles - we provide comprehensive training to all volunteers.

Medical Responder

Provide emergency medical care in disaster zones. Roles available for all certification levels from first aid to licensed medical professionals.

Requirements:
  • Medical certification (for professional roles)
  • Ability to work in high-stress environments
  • 18+ years old

Field Coordinator

Help organize relief efforts on the ground, manage supplies, and coordinate between teams and local authorities.

Requirements:
  • Strong organizational skills
  • Leadership experience
  • Ability to travel

Remote Support

Assist with logistics, communications, data entry, and other critical functions from anywhere in the world.

Requirements:
  • Reliable internet connection
  • Basic computer skills
  • 4+ hours weekly commitment

Translation Specialist

Provide translation services for affected communities and responders (written or spoken).

Requirements:
  • Fluency in 2+ languages
  • Cultural sensitivity
  • Availability for on-call shifts

Logistics Volunteer

Help manage and transport supplies, set up distribution centers, and maintain equipment.

Requirements:
  • Physical ability to lift 50lbs
  • Valid driver's license (for some roles)
  • Flexible schedule

Community Support

Provide emotional support, help reunite families, and assist with temporary shelter operations.

Requirements:
  • Compassion and empathy
  • Good communication skills
  • Background check required

Volunteer FAQ

What is the time commitment for volunteers?

Most roles require a minimum commitment of 8 hours per month, though this varies by position. Crisis response volunteers may need to be available for extended shifts during active disasters. Remote support roles often have more flexible scheduling.

Do I need prior experience to volunteer?

While some specialized roles require specific qualifications, many positions are open to volunteers with no prior experience. We provide comprehensive training for all volunteers based on their role and skill level.

Are there age restrictions for volunteers?

Most field positions require volunteers to be at least 18 years old. Some remote support roles may be available to younger volunteers with parental consent. Senior volunteers are welcome and valued!

Will I need to travel as a volunteer?

Only if you choose to! Many roles are location-specific or can be done remotely. For those interested in deployment, we offer opportunities to join response teams both domestically and internationally (travel expenses covered).

How quickly can I start volunteering after applying?

The onboarding process typically takes 2-4 weeks, depending on the role and required training. Some crisis response positions may have accelerated onboarding during active disasters.
